Tonkotsu Ramen

"Ramen, or more exactly, tonkotsu ramen, is the best thing to make at home.

Top everything with a ramen egg and some chashu pork slices, and you'll be in ramen heaven in no time.

Anyone can make this taste-buds exploding, umami-abundant, bowl of noodles... even if you live in Alaska.

I know it's not the traditional way, but look on the bright side - now you can make RAMEN, at home (sort of)!"

Ingredients:

Note: Everywhere you see vinegar - preferably, use apple cider or rice vinegar, as those don't have as harsh of a flavor as regular vinegar.

Broth:

  • 3 pounds of pig feet - or any bones and skins from large animals (pork, cow, deer, goat, moose, etc)
  • A bunch of green onions
  • 6 inch knob of ginger
  • 2 yellow onions
  • 1 garlic bulb
  • 1 red onion

Tare:

  • 1 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup mirin/mirin substitute
  • 1 tsp anchovie paste/fillet
  • 2 roasted garlic bulbs

Chashu Pork:

  • 3-4 lbs of pork belly (preferably with the skin on)
  • 3/4 cups soy sauce
  • 3/4 cups mirin
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 6 inch knob of ginger
  • 6 garlic cloves
  • A bunch of green onion

Ramen Egg:

  • 4 soft boiled eggs
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup mirin
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/8 cup vinegar

Mirin Substitute

  • 4 tbsp dry white wine
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 2/3 tbsp vinegar

Steps/Methods:

  1. Blanch the pig feet.
  2. Char the veggies/vegetables
  3. Add the veggies and pig feet into a pot and boil for 12 hours (making sure to stir once every 15 minutes for the next 90 minutes, and to also refill the water every hour)
  4. Tightly tie the pork belly, put it into a small pot or deep kitchen pan with the, hopefully, pre-cut veggies, and bake in the oven for 3-4 hours at 350F
  5. Make the tare, by mixing all the ingredients above
  6. Boil the eggs for 6 and half minutes uncovered, then transfer them to a water bath to cool
  7. Soak the eggs in the aromatic sauce made with the liquids in it's ingredients, listed above. Minimum soak is 4 hours, but ideally, you'd want to soak for 12
